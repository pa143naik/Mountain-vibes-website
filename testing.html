Testing

Testing of soil, water, food, and medicine is essential to ensure their safety, quality, and nutrient content. These tests help detect harmful substances like chemicals, bacteria, or toxins that could pose risks to human health. In agriculture, soil and water testing supports better crop production by identifying nutrient levels and guiding the use of fertilizers. Food and medicine are tested to confirm they meet health standards and are safe for consumption or use. These practices play a vital role in protecting public health, improving agricultural output, and safeguarding the environment. Testing can be carried out in laboratories or directly in the field using portable testing kits

FOOD

Food testing in a medical context primarily focuses on identifying food allergies and intolerances, assessing food safety, and evaluating nutritional content.

SOIL

Soil testing helps evaluate nutrient levels, detect harmful substances, and assess soil health. This supports better crop growth and environmental protection.

WATER

Water testing helps analyze chemical and biological content, detect pollutants, and assess quality. This ensures safe consumption and protects the environment.

MEDICIEN

Medicine testing helps identify chemical composition, detect contaminants, and quality. This supports safe usage, effective treatment, and public health protection.
